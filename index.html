Legal Protections

Know Your Legal Rights

Sri Lanka has laws to protect you. Understanding them empowers you to act with confidence.

⚖️ Penal Code Section 345
Sexual harassment is a criminal offence under Section 345 of the Sri Lanka Penal Code. It covers harassment using assault, criminal force, or words/actions that cause annoyance. Perpetrators can be arrested and prosecuted.
🛡️ Right to Complain Anonymously
You have the right to file a complaint without revealing your identity. The NTC accepts anonymous reports via email at chairman@ntc.gov.lk. The Police CWB also protects your identity during investigation.
🏥 Right to Counselling
You are entitled to free psychological counselling through Women In Need (WIN) Sri Lanka. Trauma counselling is available regardless of whether you file a formal police complaint.
📋 Right to Legal Representation
WIN Sri Lanka provides free legal services to survivors of gender-based violence, including representation in court. You do not need to face the legal process alone.
🚫 Right Against Victim Blaming
No officer has the right to question your clothing, behaviour, or choices in a way that implies fault. If you experience this from police, you can escalate your complaint to the DIG in charge of the CWB.
🏠 Emergency Shelter Rights
If you feel unsafe at home due to retaliation after reporting, WIN Sri Lanka provides emergency shelter. Call their 24-hour hotline at 011-7762517 to access this service confidentially.
